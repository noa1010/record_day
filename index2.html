Step 5- Present the

presentation

Master the material
▶

While you don't write down everything you say on the slides, it is essential that you master the material well. Practice several times, this way you will be able to say what is on each slide, even without having to read.

Eye contact
▶

Make eye contact with the audience. This builds trust and engagement. Try scanning your room for different people throughout the presentation.

Tone and Pace
▶

Speak in a clear tone and at a pace that allows the audience to follow. Incorporate changes in tone and pace to avoid monotony and maintain interest.

Body Language
▶

Stand firm and confident. Use natural hand gestures to emphasize points, but avoid nervous or aimless movements. Don’t turn your back to the audience to read from the slide – remember, the slide is just a reference point.

Audience Interaction
▶

Ask questions, encourage questions from the audience, and if appropriate, incorporate short exercises or discussions. This breaks up the routine and increases the level of engagement.

Strong Beginnings and Ends
▶

Open your presentation with a “hook” – a surprising statement, an interesting question, or a short story that will grab the audience’s attention. End with a clear summary of the main points and a “call to action” or key message that you want the audience to take away.
